# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free project for everyone, regardless of experience level, identity, background, disability, appearance, nationality, religion, or technical preference.

## Expected behavior

Participants should:

- communicate with empathy and technical clarity;
- critique ideas and code, not people;
- respect differing device constraints and levels of Android experience;
- provide reproducible evidence when making technical claims;
- accept maintainers' decisions about scope, safety, and project direction;
- protect private information found in logs, screenshots, or reports;
- help keep discussions focused and constructive.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable:

- harassment, insults, threats, discrimination, or sexualized conduct;
- publishing private information without permission;
- deliberately misleading security or compatibility claims;
- repeated off-topic promotion, spam, or disruption;
- pressuring users to grant privileged access without explaining risk;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove comments, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate this policy. Responses may include a warning, temporary restriction, or permanent ban, depending on severity and pattern of behavior.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository maintainer through an appropriate private GitHub channel. Security vulnerabilities must be reported according to `SECURITY.md`, not through a public conduct report.

Maintainers will make a reasonable effort to protect the privacy of reporters and affected participants.

## Scope

This code applies in repository spaces and when someone is publicly representing the project, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and project-related community channels.
